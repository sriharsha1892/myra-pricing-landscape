How our space prices, and where the expert sits

myRA is sold primarily per seat. These are the companies in the same space, how each one prices, and where it puts the expert.

The shape of the field
Per seatMost of the field still prices per seat or per access, with the AI bundled into the subscription to protect renewals.
Per deliverableA smaller, faster-growing set prices the finished deliverable, the report or the review, not the login.
The expertSits anywhere from invisible, to embedded in the service, to the product being sold.
Our lanePriced per deliverable with the expert reviewing it. It exists in the field, but it is thin.

The short version

What the scan shows, in five lines

The closer a company is to what myRA does, the more it charges per finished deliverable and puts a named expert on it. Across the wider field, though, per-seat with no named expert is still the norm.

Caveats

Limits of this read

What the scan can and cannot tell you. These are limits of the data, not advice.

Method. A June-2026 deep-research scan, curated to the companies that ship a research deliverable. Off-thesis entries were removed: CX and support agents, sales bots, brand-monitoring tools, transcript-only expert networks, generic horizontal research agents, and the broad research incumbents. Mercor is retained as a expert-supply cost reference and flagged in its card. The expert layer is shown as a column in the matrix view. The companion workbook carries the full records.
Confidence and gaps. Figures are reproduced only where the source carried them, blank otherwise. No revenue, funding or accuracy figure was invented. The set carries no independent software reviews, so any quality signal is funding-implied or self-reported. Geographies beyond the US, UK and EU returned ecosystem noise rather than named products and are under-represented.
The read. Funded peers show a move off per-seat exists in the field, but the set over-represents per-deliverable players by design and carries no independent reviews, so it shows what exists, not what works. The constraint visible across the close peers is reviewer supply, not the pricing design.
